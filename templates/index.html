Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about TCAS preparation and our platform

What is TCAS and how does it work?

TCAS (Thai University Central Admission System) is the centralized university admission system in Thailand. It consists of multiple rounds of applications where students can apply to various universities and programs based on their qualifications and exam scores. Each round has different criteria and requirements.

How early should I start preparing for TCAS?

We recommend starting your TCAS preparation at least 12-18 months before your intended application date. This gives you enough time to understand the requirements, prepare for the necessary exams, and build a strong portfolio if required by your chosen programs.

Do you offer scholarships or financial assistance?

Yes, we offer scholarship opportunities for high-performing students and those with financial needs. Additionally, we have flexible payment plans and group discounts. Contact our support team for more information about financial assistance options.

Can I access the course materials after completing the program?

Yes, all enrolled students retain access to course materials for a full year after the completion of their program. You can review lessons, practice tests, and resources anytime during this period.

How are your practice tests different from other preparation materials?

Our practice tests are developed by experienced educators and former university admission officers who understand the exact format and difficulty level of actual TCAS exams. We regularly update our question banks based on the latest exam patterns and provide detailed explanations for each answer to enhance your understanding.
